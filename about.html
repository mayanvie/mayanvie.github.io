About

Learning & Talent Operations · Ho Chi Minh City

I'm moving into Learning & Talent Operations because I keep seeing the same pattern. People get stuck when the path isn't clear. Sometimes asking for help feels worse than staying quiet. I want to work on that part of the job, not only the slides.

My path (short version)

I studied Cultural Studies at USSH (Ho Chi Minh City). That trained me to notice context. Who someone is. What actually matters to them. Why a reasonable instruction still fails in real life.

After that: content and marketing at a creative startup (many hats), guiding visitors at art exhibitions, private tutoring, and assisting at an English center. Different jobs, same pattern underneath. Someone is new, overloaded, or afraid of getting it wrong. I try to make the next step obvious.

Now I'm aiming for Learning Operations, Talent & Learning Operations, and learning experience design in edtech, creative teams, or corporate L&D. That's the direction I'm building toward.
